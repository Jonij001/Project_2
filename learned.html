WVS analytic trail

WVS is a global sociological research project with the purpose to explore people’s values and beliefs over time. We want to look at how the use of personal computers have changed in frequency throughout the years. In the survey the question “How often use of PC/personal computer” was only asked between the years 2005-2009 (Wave 5) and 2010-2014 (Wave 6) making these our datasets to examan.

We select the largest dataset (in form of countries) possible that can be found in both Wave 5 and 6.

In the map overview we filter on the index where people answered “frequently” to the question. Here we see that the frequency for all countries increased from Wave 5 to Wave 6 as the index values got higher.

When filtering on a higher index, displaying only the countries that most frequently uses PCs, a trend could be seen in both Wave 5 and 6 where Sweden had the highest index. This made me interested in more closely looking at Sweden.

We then Zoom in at Sweden with a more detailed view.

We decide to filter on age to see in what age groups the frequency increased the most between Wave 5 and 6. Here we see that most of the younger generation already frequently used PCs in Wave 5 with only a small increase in Wave 6. Only 53% of the ones being 50 years old or higher used PCs frequently in Wave 5 with a more significant increase in Wave 6.
